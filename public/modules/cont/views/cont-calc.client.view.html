Fuel Savings Calculator

One factor that should play an important role in your decision to buy a car is how much it will cost to drive it. For example, a vehicle that gets 30 MPG will cost you $573 less to fuel each year than one that gets 20 MPG (assuming 15000 miles of driving annually and a fuel cost of $2.29). In addition to saving you money at the gas pump, getting better gas mileage helps to save money in a number of other ways, such as reducing oil dependence costs on our economy and increasing energy sustainability. This tool will allow you to consider the costs of two vehicles with different miles per gallon, or gas mileage. It will also show you what your monthly and annual savings would be with the car which has better gas mileage, as well as what the savings would be over the foreseeable future for the next ten years.
